What is Ruby?

Ruby is a programming language known for its simplicity and productivity. It was designed and developed in the mid-1990s by Yukihiro Matsumoto in Japan. Ruby is an object-oriented language and is known for its elegant syntax, which is easy to read and write. It is often used for web development and is the language behind the popular web application framework Ruby on Rails.

Ruby is also known for its focus on developer happiness and productivity, with a strong emphasis on human-friendly design and a philosophy of “optimizing for programmer joy.” The language has a strong and supportive community, and it is often used for rapid prototyping and web development due to its flexibility and ease of use.
